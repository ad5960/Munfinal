Pentagon

General Guidelines

  • Pentagon is a multi-disciplinary competition with five different subclasses of events.
  • Participants submit their entry in a subclass of their choice where the entry is then deliberated and voted upon by judges as well as the audience.
  • The theme for our event is The Old World: Our Homeland in Transition.
  • All subclasses have pre-determined prompts that surround the aforementioned theme.
  • All entries must adhere to the general guidelines of their subclasses.
  • One cannot register in more than one subclass. If one does do so, the latest subclass will be considered as the final choice.
  • Similarly, one cannot make more than one entry. If one does do so, the latest entry will be considered as the main entry.
  • The Registrations close on the 6th December, 2021 at 4:00pm.
  • Once the registration closes, all entries will be analysed by anonymous judges.
  • Top candidates will be nominated for online voting on the website.
  • Winners of every subclass will be determined based on their votes.
  • All entries must be in English and must follow all the criterias. Inability to do so will result in disqualification of the entry.

    • Kodak (Photography)

  • Prompt: Sounds of Silence - Even the loudest of all find themselves silenced by the shutter.
  • The photograph must be taken by the participant.
  • The photograph must have a connection to the aforementioned prompt.
  • Participants must submit a PDF with the following information:
  • Title
  • Description of the shot.
  • Photograph.
  • Acceptable format of entry: PDF of maximum size 10MB.

    • Gutenberg (Op-Ed)

  • Prompt: Who we aren't - What could we be and what will we never be?
  • Word limit: 350-400words
  • The Op-Ed should be connected to the prompt and be an original piece of writing.
  • Plagiarism will lead to direct disqualification.
  • Participants must submit a PDf with the following information:
  • Title
  • Article
  • Acceptable format for entry - PDF.

    • Words' Worth(Poetry)

  • Prompt: Leaving it behind - When all is lost, the future remains.
  • The poem should be connected to the prompt and be an original piece of writing.
  • Moreover, plagiarism will lead to direct disqualification.
  • Acceptable format for entry- Adobe PDF.
  • Participants must submit a PDF with the following information:
  • Title
  • Article

    • Etch-A-Sketch (Digital Art)

  • Prompt: All shapes and sizes - Shaping tomorrow with pieces of yesterday.
  • The artwork must be an original piece of work.
  • In case one takes inspiration from another artwork, it must be attached in the PDF.
  • The illustration must have a connection to the aforementioned prompt.
  • Participants must submit a pdf with the following information:
  • Title
  • Description of the artwork(60-100 words)
  • 3 screenshots of the process.
  • Final artwork.
  • Acceptable format of entry: PDF of maximum size 10MB.

    • Art Attack (Fine Art)

  • Prompt: A Passage to India - An ode to the land of diversity and desires.
  • Acceptable format of entry: PDF of maximum size 10MB.
  • Fine art comprises the following :
  • Pencil Drawing
  • Watercolour Painting
  • Acrylic Painting
  • Oil Painting
  • Pastel Painting
  • Paper Craft
  • The artwork must be an original piece of work.
  • Participants are required to scan the artwork and submit it.
  • Participants must submit a PDF with the following information:
  • Title
  • Description of the artwork (60-100 words)
  • 3 photographs of the process.
  • Final artwork.
